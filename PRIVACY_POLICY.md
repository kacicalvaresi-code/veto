## 3. How Veto Works Without Your Data

Veto achieves powerful spam blocking through on-device processing and privacy-preserving techniques:

1.  **On-Device Blocklist:** Your personal list of blocked numbers is stored securely in your device's local storage. When a call comes in, the operating system (iOS or Android) checks it against this local list.
2.  **Anonymous Reporting:** When you choose to report a number, the app sends **only the spam phone number** to our backend proxy. This report is completely anonymous and is not linked to you or your device in any way. We use this anonymized data to strengthen our community blocklist.

---

## 4. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is created and stored exclusively on your device. You have full control over this data and can clear it at any time from the app's Settings screen.

- **Your Blocklist:** The list of phone numbers you have chosen to block.
- **Your Audit Log:** A history of calls and texts that Veto has blocked or that you have reported.
- **Your Usage Metrics:** Aggregate counts of blocked calls and texts, displayed on your dashboard.

This data is sandboxed within the Veto application and is not accessible by other apps, except for the necessary operating system extensions (Call Directory Extension on iOS, Call Screening Service on Android) that perform the blocking.

---

## 5. Third-Party Services

Veto utilizes one third-party service for its community reporting feature:

- **Call Control API:** When you report a spam number, it is anonymously submitted to the Call Control database to help identify emerging spam campaigns. No personal data is sent with this report. You can review their privacy policy at `https://www.callcontrol.com/privacy-policy`.

We do not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services.

## 6. Your Rights Under GDPR & CCPA

Data privacy regulations like the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grant you rights over your personal data, such as the right to access, correct, or delete it.

Because Veto collects **no personal data**, these rights are inherently fulfilled by our architecture. There is no data to access, correct, or delete from our servers because we do not have any.
